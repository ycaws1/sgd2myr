  • Depending on your plan, the amount you're exchanging, and the day of the week, you may pay a currency exchange fee. If you're sending SGD to a MYR account, you may also pay a small transfer fee. You'll always see the total cost in-app before you exchange, along with the Revolut exchange rate.

    Currency exchange fees

    Depending on your plan, you may pay a fee on any exchanges you make during weekends, between 17:00 Eastern Time (ET) on Fridays and 18:00 ET on Sundays. You'll pay a 1% fee on the Standard plan, and a 0.5% fee on the Plus plan. You won't pay any weekend fees on our Premium, Metal, or Ultra plans.

    If you're over your monthly exchange allowance, you'll pay an additional fair usage fee, depending on your plan (1% for the Standard plan and 0.5% for the Plus plan). There are no fair usage fees with our Premium, Metal, and Ultra plans. These limits are set on a rolling monthly basis. You can check your fair usage limit and the date it will reset under Your plan in your profile.

    Money transfer fees

    When you send SGD to a MYR account, you may pay a small transfer fee. This is the amount we charge to process your transfer. The total cost depends on the transfer method you choose, the destination country, and the amount you're sending, but don't worry — you'll see all costs live in-app before you send. You'll get discounts on these fees with our Premium, Metal, and Ultra plans.

    Heads up — external banks may charge non-Revolut transfer fees that we can't foresee.

    If you're looking for fee-free money transfers, check out our Revolut-to-Revolut transfers. You can send money to other Revolut friends in any of the 36 currencies you hold, without having to convert.

    For more information on your plan's fees, check out our terms and policies. Fees are subject to change, but you'll always see them in-app before you convert.

  • Although MYR isn't yet one of the 36 supported currencies that you can keep and exchange in-app, you can still convert SGD to MYR with us in 2 ways: by paying in Malaysian Ringgits with your multi-currency Revolut card while you're abroad or sending a money transfer to Malaysia.

    Card payments abroad

    International money transfers

    1. Download Revolut for Android or iOS, if you haven't already, and sign up for an account.
    2. Add money to your account in SGD.
    3. Go to Transfers in your Revolut app.
    4. Choose your destination country, then enter how much you want to send in SGD or how much you want your recipient to get in MYR.
    5. Check our exchange rates, fees, and estimated delivery times in-app.
    6. Fill in your recipient's details. You'll usually need their full name, BIC or SWIFT code, and their account number.
    7. Tap Send and track your transfer in-app.
